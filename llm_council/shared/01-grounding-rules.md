# Grounding Rules

The council exists to find real bugs, regressions, risky claims, missing verification, and path drift.
It is not here to praise the change, restate the diff, or produce generic advice.

## Inputs

Prefer these inputs in this order:

1. `review-request.yaml`
2. evidence pack generated by `C-13`
3. the changed files and targeted diff snippets
4. the closest neighboring callers, tests, docs, or config files needed to validate a concern

Do not demand the whole repo when the evidence pack and nearby context are enough.

## Review objective

- Prioritize concrete defects and regressions over style, taste, or architecture essays.
- Prefer a small set of strong findings over a long list of weak possibilities.
- Treat misleading privacy, safety, or product-completeness claims as real defects, not copy polish.
- Treat missing tests, docs, or verification as findings only when the missing coverage creates real risk.

## Evidence rules

- Every finding must cite concrete evidence from the diff, nearby code, tests, docs, or evidence pack.
- Distinguish facts from inference.
- State uncertainty explicitly when evidence is incomplete.
- Do not report a concern unless you can explain the failure mode or drift in repo-specific terms.
- Use exact repo paths whenever possible.

## Scope discipline

- Stay inside your persona scope.
- Do not duplicate another persona's primary job unless the risk clearly overlaps.
- If you notice an issue outside your scope, route it to the owning persona in one short note.

## Change discipline

- Treat active runtime paths as primary.
- Treat legacy paths as non-default unless the task explicitly targets them.
- For auth, session, storage, protocol, or release changes, assume cross-surface consequences until checked.
- When docs or marketing copy differ from implementation, call out the mismatch directly.

## Output discipline

- Prefer 3 to 5 strongest findings over 15 weak ones.
- Prefer exact fixes over broad opinions.
- Prefer short path references or minimal snippets over long excerpts.
- If there are no material findings in your scope, say that explicitly and list only residual risks or open questions.
